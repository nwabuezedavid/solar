Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) Policy

Committed to Ethical Business Practices and Sustainability

Introduction to Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

CSR refers to the way in which businesses regulate themselves to ensure that their activities positively impact society. CSR policies aim to guarantee that companies work ethically, considering human rights, as well as the social, economic, and environmental impacts of their business operations. Companies should meet, and aim to exceed, any relevant legislation, and where legislation is not available, the company should follow best practices.

RCILtd is committed to ensuring that all business undertakings are carried out ethically by adhering to this policy.

Looking After Employees

  • Compliance with the Human Rights Act 2010 and the Modern Slavery Act 2015.
  • Providing a safe working environment with proper health and safety measures in place.
  • Investing in staff training and professional development.
  • Paying employees in line with or above the National Minimum/Living Wage.
  • Offering a range of benefits, including pensions, healthcare, and personal development opportunities.

Looking After Customers

  • Focus on customer satisfaction and retention through feedback and continuous improvement.
  • Ensuring the safety and quality of our products and services.
  • Commitment to clear and transparent communication with customers.

Suppliers' Standards

  • Ensuring suppliers comply with the Modern Slavery Act 2015 and the Bribery Act 2010.
  • Paying suppliers properly and on time.
  • Supporting local suppliers and promoting ethical practices.
  • Engaging in transparent and clear communication with suppliers.

Protecting the Environment

  • Monitoring and reducing waste, reusing and recycling materials wherever possible.
  • Implementing water-saving measures and tracking water usage.
  • Encouraging staff to use public transport, carpool, or greener fuel options.
  • Ensuring that all company vehicles are as energy-efficient as possible.

Community Engagement

  • Sponsoring local charities, sports clubs, and community events.
  • Encouraging staff participation in volunteering days and charitable initiatives.
  • Hiring locally to support the surrounding community.

Measurement

Regular monitoring and assessment of CSR initiatives are essential to ensure that goals are met. We will implement systems to track the effectiveness of these initiatives and record any achievements.
